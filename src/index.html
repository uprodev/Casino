What Is a No-KYC Casino?

Are you still unfamiliar with the topic and want to know more about no-KYC casinos? Let’s dive into what they are exactly. The term KYC in crypto and banking stands for “Know Your Customer.” It refers to the procedures financial institutions need to follow to verify a person’s identity before they can perform any type of banking activity.

Institutions have KYC policies in place as a precaution. These policies protect them from the legal and financial consequences of fraud, corruption, and money laundering. However, people who gamble online through crypto casinos prefer to keep their activity private. In this case, no-KYC casinos exist so users don’t have to give up any of their personal information.

Most will only require minimal details, such as a phone number or email address. You can typically create an account with the casino by entering this information and creating a unique username and password. You’ll be able to gamble anonymously and keep the following sensitive information private:

  • Name
  • Address
  • Banking or credit card information
  • Utility bill

Keep in mind that some crypto casinos have no-KYC protocols in place to deposit money and start gambling, though they will need to verify your identity before withdrawal. It also depends on the payment method you choose with some KYC-free casinos. For example, Lucky Block is a no-KYC casino only when you use an accepted cryptocurrency for deposits and withdrawals. The site accepts fiat money as payment, but if you elect for this currency, Lucky Block does need to verify your identity.
